ORNL Enlists World’s Fastest Supercomputer to Combat Coronavirus

Researchers at DOE's ORNL have used the world's most powerful and smartest supercomputer, to identify 77 small-molecule drug compounds that might warrant further study in the fight against the SARS-CoV-2 coronavirus, which is responsible for the COVID-19 disease outbreak.

Hundreds of Families Attend Oak Ridge STEM Event

Oak Ridge’s EM program and two of its contractors, UCOR and Isotek, supported a local middle school’s science, technology, engineering, and mathematics night, which attracted 700 students and their families. The event at Jefferson Middle School was the second-annual STEM night.
Read more

Tennessee Utility Uses Quantum Tech for Cybersecurity

EPB of Chattanooga hosted top researchers in quantum physics from both the Los Alamos National Laboratory and the Oak Ridge National Laboratory in a test of a new type of cybersecurity control network regarded as far safer and less vulnerable to cyberattacks than any existing controls.
Read more

With Key Isotopes Depleted, DOE Plans Production Center

DOE has proposed an isotope production and research center at ORNL that could be important for medical, national security, and research projects. DOE said its supply of certain key enriched stable isotopes has been depleted, making the U.S. more dependent upon foreign imports for enriched stable isotopes.
Read more

ORNL Receives Four 2020 FLC Technology Transfer Awards

Three technologies and one commercialization program developed at DOE's ORNL have won National Tech Transfer Awards from the Federal Laboratory Consortium. FLC Awards recognize significant accomplishments transferring federal laboratory technologies to the marketplace.
Read more

Strata-G CEO Dan Hurst receives Advanced Energy Leadership Award

The Tennessee Advanced Energy Business Council awarded Dan Hurst, CEO and Founder of Knoxville-based Strata-G, the annual Thomas B. Ballard Advanced Energy Leadership Award during TAEBC’s Annual Meeting on Thursday, March 5, 2020, at Schneider Electric’s Nashville Hub.

New Member

ETEC welcomes Hampton Inn Oak Ridge as a new member.
Nestled in the Oak Ridge business district, Hampton Inn Oak Ridge is just over a mile from Oak Ridge Civic Center and 15 minutes from the bike trails of Windrock Park. Day trips to central Knoxville are half an hour’s drive away. There are plenty of dining options within walking distance, and they are a mile from Kroger Marketplace. WiFi and free hot breakfast is always free.
