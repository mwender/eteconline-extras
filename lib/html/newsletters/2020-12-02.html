TVA / Framatome to Load 3D-Printed Fuel Assembly Bracket in Commercial Reactor

TVA will load four new 3D-printed fuel assembly brackets at its Browns Ferry nuclear power plant next spring. The components were recently manufactured at ORNL in a joint project with TVA and Framatome, as part of the Transformational Challenge Reactor program.
Read more

NASA’s Plutonium Tours U.S. Before Heading to Mars

The plutonium-238 that powers NASA’s rovers on Mars crisscrosses the U.S. on a tour of national labs. DOE officials outlined the path and process of manufacturing the Pu-238 for the Perseverance Rover that launched in July and is already about two-thirds of the way to the Red Planet.
Read more

EM Employs Drone Technology for Safer Inspections

In October, OREM used a drone to conduct visual inspections inside and outside ORNL's 3039 stack. The chimney-like structure, built in 1950, requires periodic inspections to evaluate its structural integrity.
Read more

UT Helps to Protect and Secure New Wave of Manufacturing

The University of Tennessee has joined a new institute aimed at stopping disruptions: the Cybersecurity Manufacturing Innovation Institute, a $111-million, US DOE-backed, public-private partnership led by the University of Texas at San Antonio.
Read more

Reuse of Cleanup Equipment in Oak Ridge Saves Millions

Reuse of transport platforms — large elevator-like devices installed outside facilities that allow workers to access multiple floors — among EM cleanup projects in Oak Ridge will save nearly $3.5 million. The platforms were installed at Y-12’s Biology Complex to support deactivation activities.
Read more

ORNL Joins Effort to Make Plastic More Recyclable

While some plastics are recyclable, only 8.4% nationally in 2017, according to the EPA, are recycled. To help address this issue, researchers at DOE's ORNL are joining the Bio-Optimized Technologies to keep Thermoplastics out of Landfills and the Environment, or BOTTLE, Consortium.
