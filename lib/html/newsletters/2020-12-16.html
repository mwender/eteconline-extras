Annual Meeting & Awards Celebration
Over 200 people joined us on Friday, December 11, from 11:00-2:00 for our Virtual Annual Meeting & Awards Celebration. Using the Remo platform attendees networked and then enjoyed a program emceed by ETEC's Board Chair, Eric Abelquist.
Thank you to our sponsors, our event team that included Holloway Events Co. and Rustic Roots Creative, and to you who participated. It was not the same as our in-person event, but it was the next best thing and we appreciate your support!
ETEC is pleased to announce the 2020 award winners! Muddy Boot Awards were given to Dr. Eric Abelquist, Ron Woody, and Dr. Thomas Zacharia.
Sasha Benjamin and Joe Storch were this year’s Postma Young Professional Medal recipients.
All of these individuals have made lasting and significant impacts on the Oak Ridge community and in East Tennessee. Congratulations to them all! Below are the press releases that contain more information about these award winners.

Using GPS Technology to Remediate Former Oil Tank Farm

EM crews have laid protective soil over a 9-acre parcel at ETTP where an oil tank farm once sat, and they’re preparing 21 acres of adjacent land for soil cover to improve site drainage. The oil tank farm, which operated in the 1940s and 1950s, was used to house fuel for the former K-770 Powerhouse.

UT Research Park at Cherokee Farm Welcomes Two New Tenants

University Health Pharmacy and C2 Labs moved to the Park. ETEC Member C2 Labs, is an enterprise-wide compliance and security software company, has decades of experience in national security and highly regulated industries and moved to the Park for the numerous collaboration and growth opportunities.

Building an Exascale-Class Data Center for ORNL’s Incoming Frontier Supercomputer

Frontier will reside in the former data center of the Oak Ridge Leadership Computing Facility’s Cray XK7 Titan supercomputer; once the most powerful supercomputer in the world, it was decommissioned after 7 years of service last year. After dismantling and removing Titan's components for recycling, work began to revamp the space to accommodate Frontier’s much higher requirements for power, cooling, and structural support.
