Alexander's Energy Plan: The Dollars Are In The Details

If you’ve never heard of Senator Lamar Alexander’s "New Manhattan Project for Clean Energy," you’re hardly alone. Even as the "Green New Deal" keeps generating buzz, this recently revived 2008 proposal has been largely ignored, except by a handful of fellow senators mostly on the other side of the aisle. DOE Secretary Rick Perry expressed initial support for the plan in hearings.

How the Energy Dept. Helps Fight the Opioid Crisis

President Trump recently spoke about America's opioid crisis at the Rx Drug Abuse and Heroin Summit in Atlanta. DOE, through its work at the National Laboratories and its partnership with other federal agencies, is providing a range of advanced scientific and technological tools to combat the opioid epidemic.

Tennessee’s Triangle of Biotech Innovation

There’s a triangle of teamwork in the state of Tennessee that’s developing the future of targeted therapeutics. The UTHSC College of Medicine and Pharmacy, along with ORNL are combining efforts and expertise to create breakthrough treatments for kidney disease – an extraordinary scientific and academic effort that’s already attracting commercial biotech interest.

Workers Prepare Centrifuge Complex for Demolition

Workers are preparing to tear down a complex that will help successfully complete OREM's Vision 2020, an ambitious goal to finish all demolitions at ETTP by the end of 2020. The K-1200 Centrifuge Complex is comprised of several facilities constructed between 1975 and 1985 and spanning more than 235,000 square feet.
