Improving Isotope Supply for a Cancer-Fighting Drug

Recycled medical devices, diverted from going to a special landfill, supply the key ingredient in a drug that treats prostate cancer. Researchers at ORNL have developed and demonstrated a process to produce actinium-227 using harvested radium-226 from legacy medical devices. The devices are secured by the DOE Isotope Program.

Bio-Inspired Material Targets Oceans' Uranium Stores

Scientists have demonstrated a new bio-inspired material for an eco-friendly and cost-effective approach to recovering uranium from seawater.A research team from DOE's Oak Ridge and Lawrence Berkeley National Labs, the Univ. of California, Berkeley, and the Univ. of South Florida developed a material that selectively binds dissolved uranium with a low-cost polymer adsorbent.

Fastest-Growing Nuclear Business Is Tearing Down U.S. Plants

The fastest growing part of the nuclear industry in the U.S. involves a small but expanding group of companies that specialize in tearing reactors down faster and cheaper than ever before. When Entergy Corp. shut its Vermont nuclear plant in 2014, they planned to wait until 2068 to dismantle it with $1.2 billion in anticipated costs. Instead, Entergy sold the plant to Northstar Group Services Inc., which plans to do the job by 2026 at a much reduced cost.
